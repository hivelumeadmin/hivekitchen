## Emergent Product Principles (from Round 2 refinements)

Three operating principles crystallized out of the user's refinement pattern. These are product laws, not features.

### Principle 1 — "No approvals, only proposals"

Plans never require explicit approval. Lumi proposes; parents adjust if they want to. Silence is not consent — it's trust. Plans remain mutable until the day arrives. The word "approval" leaves the vocabulary.

**Design implication:** No accept buttons. No confirmation modals. Plans exist as the default state of the world; the parent is never required to ratify them.

### Principle 2 — "The Heart Note is sacred"

The Heart Note is a parent-to-child emotional channel. It is NEVER used for logistics, reminders, chores, or utility. It exists solely to let the child feel the lunch was prepared with love.

**Design implication:** A product-level guardrail. Future features that want to "send a message to the child" must not co-opt the Heart Note channel. This goes in the "Not X" section of the Product Concept.

### Principle 3 — "Voice enriches Lumi, not the UI"

Voice exists so parents and children can have *conversations* that teach Lumi. Voice is NOT a UI shortcut, not a command channel, not a rejection menu. It is Lumi's richest learning channel.

**Design implication:** Voice is scoped to conversational enrichment, evening chat, Heart Note dictation, and Lunch Link playback. Voice is de-scoped from anywhere it would be utility rather than conversation or emotion.

## Emergent Product Surface — "The Brief"

User proposed that daily/weekly/monthly brief summaries become a core UI surface that consolidates many retention ideas and refines profiles over time.

### Daily Brief (morning, home screen)

- Today's lunch + prep card (collapsed)
- Heart Note status (written? not yet?)
- Who's packing today (if partnered)
- Notable signals from yesterday (child feedback, leftover surplus)

### Weekly Brief (Friday afternoon / when parent logs in to plan)

- This week's lunch rotation summary
- Child feedback rollup (what worked, what didn't)
- Next week's plan — already drafted by Lumi
- Cultural/seasonal context (upcoming holiday, term end)
- Weather digest for next week's planning
- Profile refinement prompts ("I noticed X. Should I update Maya's profile?")

### Monthly Brief (first weekend of each month)

- Family flavor snapshot
- Learned preferences to confirm/lock-in
- Recipes introduced, rotated, retired
- Cultural identity lean
- Retrospective warmth — emoji highlights, standout days

### Design intent

The Brief replaces the need for settings menus, logs, and dashboards. Everything Lumi learned surfaces inside a brief at the right time — not inside a page the parent must navigate to.

**Status:** Candidate for elevation to top-level product surface in the Product Concept alongside Weekly Plan, Lunch Link, and Heart Note.

### Strategic re-framing (validated by Round 3 picks)

> **The Heart Note is the retention engine for the Lunch Link — not the emoji tap.**

>
> **New framing:** The Lunch Link exists to deliver Heart Notes → the emoji tap is a data byproduct of an emotional ritual.

### Secondary safety net

Tier 2 items #103, #106, plus age-progression #81/#82, form a **graceful-degradation layer** for Lumi's intelligence when child engagement dips. Even if direct taps decay, Lumi weights signals intelligently, shifts to conservative planning, and migrates feedback toward conversation as the child ages.

## Tension 5 — Lunch Bag Composition: Flexibility vs. Cognitive Simplicity

### The tension

A "lunch" is not a single meal — it is a **Lunch Bag** containing multiple components (Main, Snack, Fruit, Drink, Treat, cultural sides). The PRD currently treats the day's plan as one unit with implicit multi-item content (see opening scene — wrap + cucumber + sunbutter cup; FR18 speaks of "items" in the plural), but there is no first-class construct for:

- Component roles (main vs. snack vs. side)
- Per-component school-policy, allergy-scope, pantry-scope, shopping-list-scope
- Whole-bag vs. per-component rating on the Lunch Link
- Cultural-prior bag shapes
- Variable bag shape per child / per day

**The core tension:** more components = richer signal, more personalization, better match to real lunch bags across cultures. But also: more surface area, more parent manipulation, more UI, more ways to violate the "low mental load" promise.

**Where on the spectrum does Lumi sit? How does composition stay invisible while still being expressive?**

> **How many components can a Lunch Bag carry before the UI collapses, and what does Lumi do so that the parent never feels the composition complexity?**

## Tension 6 — Lunch Link Rating Granularity: Whole-bag vs. Per-component

**Decision 6.1 — Hybrid two-layer rating model**

> The Lunch Link rating has **two layers**:
> - **Layer 1 — Whole-bag emoji (primary, always).** One tap from the defined 4-emoji set rates the bag as a whole. This is the one-tap ritual — the minimum expected interaction.
> - **Layer 2 — Per-slot thumbs-up (optional, discoverable).** After the whole-bag tap, the child can optionally thumbs-up individual slots (Main / Snack / Extra). Absence of a thumbs-up on a slot is **neutral, not negative** — it carries no signal weight.
>
> The child can stop after Layer 1 and nothing is lost. Layer 2 adds fidelity when the child is engaged, without penalizing children who don't drill in.

**Why this shape wins:**

- Preserves Principle 5 ("earned through eating, not through tapping") — one tap is the floor.
- Preserves the Heart Note as the retention engine (Round 3 strategic re-framing) — rating fatigue doesn't compound because the minimum is still one tap.
- Gives Lumi directional signal always (Layer 1) and per-slot signal when available (Layer 2).
- Asymmetric incentive: thumbing up is lightweight and positive; no mechanism to thumbs-down (which would invite over-labeling negatives).
- No forced per-slot interaction — respects Boundary 1 (children signal, don't operate a system).

**Implication for FR36:**

FR36 needs to be amended to reflect the two-layer model:
- Layer 1 is the single whole-bag emoji rating (preserves original FR36 intent).
- Layer 2 is a new capability — per-slot thumbs-up as an optional augmentation.
- The "rating" now has two components, but the one-tap expectation is unchanged.

- #306 Whole-bag only (status quo) — superseded; whole-bag is now Layer 1 of the hybrid.
- #307 Forced per-slot 3-tap — rejected; too heavy a load for the child.
- #309 Age-staggered granularity — rejected; hybrid's opt-in layer makes age-staggering unnecessary. Every child gets the same interaction shape; younger children simply stop at Layer 1.
- #310 Two-tap Main + "rest?" — rejected in favor of the cleaner Layer 1 / Layer 2 separation.
- #311 Main-only rating — rejected; loses Snack/Extra signal entirely. Layer 2 recovers this when available.

## Tension 6 — Synthesis & Product-Law Outputs

### Ten structural decisions

| ID | Decision |
|----|----------|
| **6.1** | **Hybrid two-layer rating model.** Layer 1 = whole-bag 4-emoji tap (always, one-tap ritual). Layer 2 = per-slot thumbs-up (optional, opt-in). No thumbs-down. |
| **6.2** | **Layer 2 gesture = swipe-right.** No swipe-left, no thumbs-down. Absence of swipe = neutral. |
| **6.3** | **Rating-surface guardrails.** Rating never required; <5-second budget; arbitrary slot order; fits on one screen. |
| **6.4** | **Age-band presentation evolution.** Structure is age-agnostic; emoji style matures, touch targets scale for younger kids, teens get an optional text-back channel. |
| **6.5** | **Lumi's signal interpretation.** Per-slot independent weighting; silence never negative; cross-child household patterns tracked; blind-variant active learning. |
| **6.6** | **Layer 1 emoji vocabulary.** `love / like / meh / no` — emotion-based, stable, cross-cultural. |
| **6.7** | **Rating window.** Opens at lunchtime or link-view; closes at **8 PM local same day**. No backlog. |
| **6.8** | **Lunch Link surface ordering.** Heart Note → Bag preview → Layer 1 → Layer 2. Heart Note visually dominant. |
| **6.9** | **Rating decay is expected.** Heart Note engagement is the true success metric; passive channels (pantry, swaps) fill in when ratings thin. |
| **6.10** | **Session integrity.** One URI per (child, day); one-time-use; sibling-shared-device flows are child-scoped. |

### Two emergent sharpenings

1. **The one-tap ritual is the floor; per-slot thumbs-up is a ceiling.** The hybrid shape absorbs the whole-bag-vs-per-component tension without choosing one over the other. The minimum viable child interaction is preserved; richer signal is available when engagement allows.
2. **Rating decay is an accepted outcome, not a failure.** Round 3's strategic re-framing is codified at the product-law level: the Lunch Link's success is Heart Note engagement, not rating density. This frees Lumi (and the product) from rating-sustainment pressure that would otherwise invite Principle-2a-violating nudges.
